Ux-research Projects

Ever since I first started studying I went back and forward between Design and Technology.

This why I love how the two are becoming more interlinked than ever before.

Throughout the years I have come to see that my strength lies in development stages of the creative process.

Becoming a UX-design designer is a great way to further develop my ressearch skills while still actively work with interactive technolgies. I am happy to share my first results and learnings with you. Let's have look...

Bespoke


For the project bespoke design I got the opportunity to work with direct users and company clients for the first time as a UX-design student.


The project consisted of two main parts, during the first part I was assigned a user in order to practice my research and observational skills by working within the different creative stages of the design process. For this assignment the most important faces where the emphatize face as we were tasked to create a tool that allows it's users to create tailor made personal experiences to be shared with loved ones. The goal of the project was to create a card personalization tool as well as gift unwrapping experience that would connect past, present and future family generations.

After having worked on a custom-made surprise for our users it was time for the second phase of the assignment. Having formed an initial idea about the project and having gained insights from our users it was time to let go of judgements heading into the second stage namely defining our final concept.

"How might we questions" helped me better emphatize with users during the different develoment stages. Even-though it was innitially hard to get started with skecting it was a very rewarding experience that really allowed me to view the "same" solution from litterally 100 different perspectives. From this my experiments became more playful an interactive with unexpected twists for both the giver as the receiver.

The HMW phase really helped my partner and I to find a concept that balance both our design visions while fitting the the needs of the client. Created a tool that enables users to connect with past, present and future generations through card giving. I got good practice on team work and was reminded that less can be more, to not be afraid to "Kills my darlings. Great Project!

The last phase was the testing of the product, this part was the most difficult part as the more we tested the more we seemed to be "missing". During this experience I learned to better asses the overall outcome in order to prevent overkill. The project was succes and we got to present it to the members of Hallmark on the final day.

Research + Design


For the project Research for Design we got to conduct our first infield research tests.


I choose to work with a dance workout group named "Salsation". The goal of this assignment was to learn about the social demographics that might influence gym-goers to join group workouts. Another point of research was to understand if cutltural differences halted of even boosted the participants to take part in the class.

The first part of my project consisted out of a lot of field research. The reasoning behind this is that I wanted to learn more about the differences and unifying factors between the different members of the dance group. For this I used the Fly on the wall technique, furthermore I made observations and took notes from video recordings and voice notes..

I choose to work with this group because, I was seeking to learn more about the influences that active experiences have on self-development and social interactions. My curiosity lay behind finding unexpected connection between the different participants.

Having attented several of the classes really helped me gain their confidence and also helped me in emphatizing with their community I was invited to take part in extra curicular activities durin which I learned more about their drive and what makes this class som important to them.

The results of this research were surprising as my assumptions were not as I had believed when starting the project. The groups showed that their bond had little to do with direct cultural background but more about the need of feeling connected and happy. This was a great project from where I improved my documentation methods as well as learned to better disect user insights.
